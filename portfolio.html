Selected Portfolio

Snowy Mountains and Starry Sky

At 3 o'clock in the morning, I waited for the Milky Way to rise. Using a long exposure, I captured the depth of the stars and the mountains. ISO 800, exposure time 25 seconds.

Core idea: Use cool tones to highlight the purity of the snow-capped mountains, and incorporate the warm light of the stars as an accent to create a visual contrast.

night scene of the city

The photo was taken at the Bund in Shanghai. A tripod was used to stabilize the camera. An f/8 aperture was set to ensure depth of field. The symmetrical beauty of the building lights and the reflection on the water surface was captured.

Core idea: Avoid the crowds and choose a low-angle shot to make the river surface act as a "mirror", extending the depth of the scene.

Autumn Leaves

Shot at Xiangshan in Beijing. Using a 50mm prime lens, with a large aperture to blur the background, focusing on the dewdrops on the fallen leaves to highlight the detailed texture.

Core idea: Use shallow depth of field to separate the subject from the background, and the warm yellow tones convey the gentle atmosphere of autumn.
